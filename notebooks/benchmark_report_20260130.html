4. Key Findings

4.1 Scaling with Qubit Count

Classical shadows show increased variance on larger circuits (6-7 qubits). This is expected due to the 3^k scaling of shadow variance with locality k. Direct methods maintain more stable performance.

4.2 Observable Count Impact

Circuits with merged suites (180-275 observables) show classical shadows competing well due to the "measure once, estimate many" advantage. For smaller observable sets (63-100), direct methods dominate.

4.3 Application-Specific Performance

  • GHZ/Entanglement verification: Classical shadows excel at multi-qubit stabilizer estimation
  • Chemistry: Direct optimized provides best energy estimation accuracy
  • QAOA: Mixed results; shadows better for 5-qubit, direct better for 7-qubit
  • Metrology: Classical shadows show lowest error for phase sensing

5. Recommendations

  1. For production VQE/chemistry: Use Direct Optimized - most reliable accuracy
  2. For exploratory analysis (many observables): Use Classical Shadows - measure once, query later
  3. For entanglement witnesses: Classical Shadows provides efficient multi-qubit estimation
  4. For 7+ qubit systems: Consider hybrid approaches to manage shadow variance
